World University Rankings

Ranking universities is a difficult, political, and controversial practice. There are hundreds of different national and international university ranking systems, many of which disagree with each other. In this visualization we will be using the Academic Ranking of World Universities (ARWU), also known as the Shanghai Ranking. ARWU is regarded as one of the three most influential and widely observed university rankings alongside QS World University Rankings and the Times Higher Education World University Rankings. It was founded in China in 2003, making it the first global university ranking with multifarious indicators.

Where are the top universities from?

Here we have a map showing the distribution of top universities around the world and where they are located in. At a glance, it is observable that majority of the top universities come from two countries that is United States and United Kingdom. On the other side of the world the leading countries are China, Japan and Australia.

Which are the top 10 univeristies?

The following idiom shows us the top 10 ranked univeristies since 2005 based off the Shanghai Ranking. From the bump chart we can see that overall the top 10 universities have successfully managed to maintain their position as a top 10 ranked university for over a decade. Harvard University in particular is the most outstanding university as it maintains the number 1 rank for the entire duration.

How are universities ranked and compared to each other?

We will now be looking at the metrics used by the Shanghai Ranking to rank the respective universities and how they compare to another. The Shanghai Ranking highlights universities scores to ranking indicators, which measure :

- Quality of Education, with Alumni (10%)
- Quality of Faculty, with Award (20%) and HiCi (20%)
- Research Output, with N&S (20%) and PUB (20%)
- Per Capita Performance, with PCP (10%)


Explanation on indicators used:

Alumni: Alumni of an institution winning Nobel Prizes                and Fields Medals.
Award: Staff of an institution winning Nobel Prizes and               Fields Medals.
HiCi: Highly Cited Researchers.
N&S: Papers published in Nature and Science.
PUB: Papers indexed in Science Citation Index -           Expanded and Social Science Citation Index.
PCP: Per capita academic performance of an institution
